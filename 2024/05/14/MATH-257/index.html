Note for MATH 257

Main question: $A\boldsymbol x = \boldsymbol b$

1 Introduction to Vectors

1.1 Vectors

  • We use $\left[ \begin{array}{} x\newline y \newline …\end{array}\right]$ to present an vector
  • Linear Combinations: $3\boldsymbol{v}+5\boldsymbol{w}$ is a typical linear combination $c\boldsymbol{v}+d\boldsymbol{w}$ of the vetors $\boldsymbol{v}$ and $\boldsymbol{w}$

1.2 Lengths and Dot Products

  • Lengths: $||\boldsymbol{v}||=\sqrt{\boldsymbol v \cdot \boldsymbol v}=\sqrt{\sum{v_i*v_i}}$
  • Dot product: $\boldsymbol{v}\cdot \boldsymbol{w}=\sum{v_i*w_i}$, $\boldsymbol{v}$ and $\boldsymbol{w}$ have same rows.
  • Angle: angle $\theta$ between $\boldsymbol v$ and $\boldsymbol w$ has $\cos \theta = \dfrac{\boldsymbol v \cdot \boldsymbol w}{||\boldsymbol v||*||\boldsymbol w||}$

2 Matrics Elimination

2.1 Matrix

  • Rows and Columns: We called $A=\left[ \begin{array}{}1 & 2 \newline 3 & 4\newline 5 & 6\end{array}\right]$ a 3 by 2 ($3\times 2$) matrix. With $m=3$ rows and $n=2$ columns.
  • Multiplication: If $A\ce{(m\times n)}\cdot B\ce{(n\times p)}=C\ce{(m\times p)}$, then $c_{ij}=\sum_{k=1}^{n}{a_{ik}}{b_{kj}}$
  • Identity Matrix: $I$, $i_{ij}=\begin{cases}0, ~ i\not =j\newline 1, ~ i=j\end{cases}$

2.2 Elimination

$A\to U$

  • $A = LU = LDU$
    • $U$: Upper Triangular System, All $0$ for $u_{ij}, i > j$.
    • $L$: Lower Triangular System, All $0$ for $l_{ij}, i < j$.
    • $E$: Elimination matrix, $U = (\prod_0^n{E_i})A$, $L = \prod_n^0 E_i^{-1}$
    • In $A = LDU $, we riqure $u_{ii}=l_{ii}=1$ and $d_{ij}=0 ~ \ce{ for }~ i \not = j$
    • Example:
      • $\left[ \begin{array}{}2 & 8 \newline 6 & 29\end{array}\right] = \left[ \begin{array}{}1 & 0 \newline 3 & 1\end{array}\right]\left[ \begin{array}{}2 & 8 \newline 0 & 5\end{array}\right] = \left[ \begin{array}{}1 & 0 \newline 3 & 1\end{array}\right]\left[ \begin{array}{}2 & 0 \newline 0 & 5\end{array}\right]\left[ \begin{array}{}1 & 4 \newline 0 & 1\end{array}\right]$
      • $E=\begin{bmatrix}1 & 0\newline -3 & 1\end{bmatrix}$
  • Augmented Matrix: $[A ~ \boldsymbol b]=[\boldsymbol a_1 ~ … ~ \boldsymbol a_n ~ \boldsymbol b]$
  • Elimination of Augmented Matrix:
    • $[A ~ \boldsymbol b]\to [U ~ \boldsymbol b’]$
    • $A=\left[\begin{array}{}3 & 2 & 1\newline 2 & 3 & 1\newline 1 & 2 & 3\end{array}\right]$, $\boldsymbol b = \left[\begin{array}{}39\newline 34\newline 26\newline\end{array}\right]$
    • $\left[\begin{array}{}3 & 2 & 1 & 39\newline 2 & 3 & 1 & 34\newline 1 & 2 & 3 & 26\end{array}\right]\to \left[\begin{array}{}3 & 2 & 1 & 39\newline 0 & 5/3 & 1/3 & 8\newline 0 & 0 & 12/5 & 33/5\end{array}\right]$

3 Inverse & Transpose

3.1 Defination

  • Inverse
    • For a square matrix $A$, inverse matrix $A^{-1}: A^{-1}A=I$ or $AA^{-1}=I$
    • If $A$ have an $A^{-1}$, then A is invertible / non-sigular, or A is sigular.
    • For square $A(n\times n)$, $A^{-1}\iff rank(A)=n$
  • Transpose
    • $A^{T}$’s row is same as $A$’s column
  • $(AB)^{-1}=B^{-1}A^{-1}$
  • $(AB)^T=B^TA^T$
  • $(A^{-1})^T=(A^T)^{-1}$

3.2 Calculate $A^{-1}$

  • Gauss-Jordan Elimination
    • For $AA^{-1}=I$, make an Augmented Matrix with $[A ~ I]$, make elimination to $[U ~ A^{-1}]$
    • Example: $\left[\begin{array}{}1 & 3\newline 2 & 7\end{array}\right] A^{-1}=\left[\begin{array}{}1 & 0\newline 0 & 1\end{array}\right]$
      • $[A ~ I] = \left[\begin{array}{}1 & 3 & 1 & 0\newline 2 & 7 & 0 & 1\end{array}\right]$
      • Do Gauss Elimination to: $\begin{bmatrix}1 & 3 & 1 & 0\newline 0 & 1 & -2 & 1\end{bmatrix}$
      • Do Jordan Elimination to:$\begin{bmatrix}1 & 0 & 7 & 3\newline 0 & 1 & -2 & 1\end{bmatrix} = [I ~ A^{-1}]$
      • So $A^{-1}=\begin{bmatrix} 7 & -3\newline -2 & 1\end{bmatrix}$

4 Space

4.1 Definition

  • All linear combination of all vectors in Space(or Subspace) will still in Space(or subspace).
  • $\ce{Subspace} ~ R^m \subset \ce{Space} ~ R^n$, We called $R^m$ is a subspace of $R^n$, $n\ge m$ obviously.
  • The Space $R^n$ consists of all vectors $\boldsymbol v$ with $n$ components
  • $\boldsymbol v_1, \boldsymbol v_2, …,\boldsymbol v_n\in R^m$, for any $\boldsymbol w = \sum_{i=1}^n k_i \boldsymbol v_i (k_i ~ \ce{is any real number})$,$\boldsymbol w\in ~ {R^m}$. ${R^m}$ can be space or subspace.
  • Point $[0,…,0]^T$ in any Space and any Subspace

4.2 Span

  • The space $span{\boldsymbol v_1, \boldsymbol v_2, …, \boldsymbol v_n}$ is by all linear combination of these $n$ vectors, it must be $R^n$ or a subspace of $R^n$
  • Example:
    • $span{[1,1]^T,[1,1]^T}=R^1(y=x)$
    • $span{[1,1]^T,[1,2]^T}=R^2$

4.3 independence, basis, rank and dimension

  • Linear independent: $\sum{c_i\boldsymbol{x}_i}\not ={0}$ for any $c_i$ with $\prod c_i\not = 0$
  • basis: basis are linear independent. Basis is not unique.
  • rank
    • Defination: The rank of $A$ is the number of pivots. This number is $r$ / $\ce{r}(A)$ / $\ce{rank}(A)$. Also the dimension of $C(A)$ and $C(A^T)$. ($\ce{rank}(A)=\ce{rank}(A^T)$)
    • Pivots and Free variables:
      • For $A ~ (n\times m)$:
        • The numbers of Pivots = $\ce{rank}(A)$
        • The numbers of Free variables = $m - \ce{rank}(A)$
  • dimension: $A$ has dimension $\ce{rank}(A)$

4.4 Spaces of Matrix

  • Example: $A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 2\newline 2 & 4\newline 3 & 5\end{bmatrix}$
  • $C(A)=\ce{span}{[1,2,3]^T,[2,4,5]^T}$: Column Space of A
    • $C(A)=C([\boldsymbol v_1, \boldsymbol v_2, …, \boldsymbol v_n])=\ce{span}{\boldsymbol v_1, \boldsymbol v_2, …, \boldsymbol v_n}$
  • $C(A^T)=\ce{span}{[1,2]^T,[3,5]^T}$: Row Space of A
    • $C(A^T)=C(\begin{bmatrix}-\boldsymbol v_1-\newline -\boldsymbol v_2-\newline ……\newline -\boldsymbol v_n-\end{bmatrix})=\ce{span}{\boldsymbol v_1, \boldsymbol v_2, …, \boldsymbol v_n}$
  • $N(A)=R^0$: Nullspace of A
    • All solutions $\boldsymbol x$ to $A\boldsymbol{x}=0$
  • $N(A^T)=\ce{span}{[-2,1,0]^T}$: Left Nullspace of A
    • All solutions $\boldsymbol x$ to $A^T\boldsymbol{x}=0$
  • For $A(n\times m)$, $\begin{cases} \ce{rank}(C(A))+\ce{rank}(N(A^T))=n, C(A)\perp N(A^T)\newline \ce{rank}(C(A^T))+\ce{rank}(N(A))=m, C(A^T)\perp N(A)\end{cases}$

4.5 Solve $A \boldsymbol x = \boldsymbol b$

  • First, solve $A \boldsymbol x = \boldsymbol 0~(N(A))$
    • $R$: Reduced Row Echelon form $R = \ce{rref}(A)$
      • $N(A)=N(U)=N(R)$
      • has all pivots = $1$, with zeros above and below
    • We can use Elimination or $R$ to solve it.
  • Then, solve $A \boldsymbol x = \boldsymbol b$
    • $\boldsymbol{x}{complete}=\boldsymbol{x}{particular}+\ce{any linear combination of N(A)}$
